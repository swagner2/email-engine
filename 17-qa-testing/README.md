# Quality Assurance (QA) Testing Module

This module provides a comprehensive framework for quality assurance testing of all email campaigns and flows. It ensures that every email sent is error-free, on-brand, and optimized for performance.

---

## Overview

Email marketing mistakes can be costly. A single typo, broken link, or rendering issue can damage brand reputation and lead to lost revenue. This QA Testing Module provides a structured, repeatable process for catching and fixing errors before they reach your subscribers' inboxes.

---

## What's Included

### 1. QA Testing Workflow Framework

**File:** `FRAMEWORK_QA-Testing-Workflow.md`

This document outlines the four-phase QA workflow that should be followed for every email campaign and flow:

1. **Setup & Strategy Verification**
2. **Content & Design QA**
3. **Technical QA**
4. **Final Approval & Scheduling**

It also defines roles and responsibilities and includes flow-specific QA considerations.

### 2. Campaign QA Checklist

**File:** `CHECKLIST_Campaign-QA.md`

A comprehensive, printable checklist for QA testing email campaigns. It covers all four phases of the QA workflow and includes space for notes and sign-off.

### 3. Flow QA Checklist

**File:** `CHECKLIST_Flow-QA.md`

A comprehensive, printable checklist for QA testing email flows. It includes both flow-level checks (triggers, filters, time delays) and email-level checks for each email in the flow.

### 4. Troubleshooting Guide

**File:** `GUIDE_Troubleshooting-Common-Issues.md`

A quick reference guide for diagnosing and fixing the most common issues encountered during email QA, including rendering issues, personalization errors, link problems, deliverability issues, and flow-specific problems.

## How to Use This Module

### For New Campaigns

1. Review the `FRAMEWORK_QA-Testing-Workflow.md` to understand the four-phase process.
2. Print or open the `CHECKLIST_Campaign-QA.md`.
3. Work through each phase of the checklist, checking off items as you go.
4. If you encounter any issues, consult the `GUIDE_Troubleshooting-Common-Issues.md`.
5. Obtain final sign-off before scheduling the campaign.

### For New Flows

1. Review the `FRAMEWORK_QA-Testing-Workflow.md`, paying special attention to the flow-specific considerations.
2. Print or open the `CHECKLIST_Flow-QA.md`.
3. Complete Part 1 (Flow-Level QA) first.
4. Then, complete Part 2 (Email-Level QA) for each email in the flow.
5. If you encounter any issues, consult the `GUIDE_Troubleshooting-Common-Issues.md`.
6. Perform a live trigger test with a test profile.
7. Obtain final sign-off before activating the flow.

### For Ongoing Management

- Use the checklists for every campaign and flow, no exceptions.
- Document any recurring issues and work to address them at the root cause level.
- Regularly review and update the QA process based on learnings.

## Best Practices

- **Make QA Mandatory:** The QA process should be a required step in your email production timeline, not optional.
- **Involve Multiple People:** A fresh pair of eyes can often catch mistakes that the original creator missed.
- **Use Tools:** Leverage tools like Litmus, Email on Acid, and automated link checkers to streamline the process.
- **Document Everything:** Keep a record of all QA tests, issues found, and resolutions.
- **Don't Rush:** Quality takes time. Build adequate QA time into your project timelines.
