> This library contains a comprehensive list of A/B test ideas organized by category. Use this as a reference when planning your testing calendar or when you need inspiration for your next test.

# A/B Testing Ideas Library

---

## 1. Subject Line Tests

### Personalization
- First name vs. no first name
- Location-based personalization vs. generic
- Purchase history reference vs. generic

### Length
- Short (< 30 characters) vs. long (> 50 characters)
- Single word vs. full sentence

### Tone & Style
- Curiosity-driven vs. benefit-driven
- Question vs. statement
- Formal vs. casual
- Urgent vs. calm

### Emojis
- With emoji vs. without emoji
- Single emoji vs. multiple emojis
- Emoji at beginning vs. end

### Content Type
- Product name vs. benefit
- Discount amount vs. "special offer"
- Scarcity ("Only 3 left") vs. urgency ("Ends tonight")
- Social proof ("Join 10,000 customers") vs. no social proof

---

## 2. Preview Text Tests

- Reinforcing subject line vs. adding new information
- Urgency message vs. value proposition
- Call to action vs. descriptive text
- With emoji vs. without emoji

---

## 3. Sender Name Tests

- Company name vs. person's name
- CEO/Founder name vs. generic "Team"
- Department name (e.g., "Customer Success Team") vs. company name
- First name only vs. first and last name

---

## 4. Call to Action (CTA) Tests

### Copy
- Action-oriented ("Shop Now") vs. benefit-oriented ("Get My Discount")
- Urgent ("Buy Now") vs. casual ("Check It Out")
- First person ("Get My Deal") vs. second person ("Get Your Deal")
- Specific ("Shop Dresses") vs. generic ("Shop Now")

### Design
- Button color (test 2-4 colors)
- Button size (large vs. small)
- Button shape (rounded vs. square)
- Text-only link vs. button

### Placement
- Above the fold vs. below the fold
- Single CTA vs. multiple CTAs
- CTA at top, middle, and bottom vs. only at bottom

---

## 5. Email Layout & Design Tests

### Structure
- Single column vs. multi-column
- Image-heavy vs. text-heavy
- Long-form storytelling vs. short and punchy
- Plain text vs. HTML design

### Visual Hierarchy
- Hero image at top vs. headline at top
- Large product image vs. small product image
- Full-width image vs. contained image

### Content Order
- Product first vs. story first
- Discount/offer at top vs. at bottom
- Social proof at top vs. at bottom

---

## 6. Image Tests

### Type
- Lifestyle photography vs. product-only shots
- Model wearing product vs. product on white background
- Close-up vs. full shot
- Single product vs. multiple products

### Presence
- With image vs. without image
- GIF vs. static image
- Video thumbnail vs. static image

---

## 7. Copy & Messaging Tests

### Length
- Short copy (< 50 words) vs. long copy (> 200 words)
- Bullet points vs. paragraphs

### Tone
- Formal vs. casual
- Playful vs. serious
- Educational vs. promotional

### Focus
- Product features vs. product benefits
- Customer testimonial vs. brand messaging
- Problem-focused vs. solution-focused

### Personalization
- Generic greeting vs. personalized greeting
- Generic product recommendations vs. personalized recommendations
- No personalization vs. dynamic content based on behavior

---

## 8. Flow Timing & Duration Tests

### Welcome Series
- 3 emails vs. 5 emails
- Immediate first email vs. delayed by 1 hour
- Daily cadence vs. every other day

### Abandoned Cart
- 1 hour, 12 hours, 24 hours vs. 2 hours, 24 hours, 48 hours
- 2-email sequence vs. 4-email sequence
- Immediate discount vs. delayed discount

### Post-Purchase
- 1 day, 7 days, 14 days vs. 3 days, 10 days, 21 days
- Cross-sell immediately vs. wait 7 days

### Win-Back
- 60 days vs. 90 days vs. 120 days
- 2 emails vs. 4 emails

### Back-in-Stock
- Immediate alert vs. 4-hour delay
- Single email vs. reminder after 24 hours

---

## 9. Offer & Incentive Tests

### Type
- Percentage discount (10% off) vs. dollar amount ($10 off)
- Free shipping vs. discount
- Gift with purchase vs. discount
- Contest entry vs. discount

### Amount
- 10% off vs. 15% off vs. 20% off
- $5 off vs. $10 off vs. $20 off

### Presentation
- Discount code vs. automatic discount
- Countdown timer vs. no timer
- Minimum purchase requirement vs. no minimum

---

## 10. Social Proof Tests

### Type
- Customer review vs. star rating
- Number of customers ("Join 10,000 customers") vs. percentage ("95% recommend")
- Testimonial with photo vs. without photo
- Press mention vs. customer testimonial

### Placement
- Top of email vs. middle vs. bottom
- Near product vs. near CTA

---

## 11. Segmentation Tests

### Audience
- New customers vs. returning customers
- High AOV vs. low AOV
- Engaged subscribers vs. less engaged

### Content
- Personalized content for segment A vs. generic content
- Different products for different segments

---

## 12. Send Time Tests

- Morning (8-10am) vs. afternoon (12-2pm) vs. evening (6-8pm)
- Weekday vs. weekend
- Optimal send time (Klaviyo AI) vs. fixed time

---

## 13. Multi-Channel Tests

### Email + SMS
- Email first, then SMS vs. SMS first, then email
- Email only vs. email + SMS
- Plain text SMS vs. image-based SMS

---

## 14. Form Tests

### Headline
- Benefit-led ("Get 10% Off") vs. curiosity-led ("Unlock a Surprise")
- Short headline vs. long headline

### Offer
- Percentage discount vs. free shipping
- Discount vs. contest entry
- 10% off vs. 15% off

### Trigger Timing
- Immediate vs. 10 seconds vs. 30 seconds
- Scroll trigger (50% vs. 75%) vs. exit-intent
- Time on page (30 seconds vs. 60 seconds)

### Form Fields
- Email only vs. email + name
- Single-step vs. multi-step form

### Design
- With image vs. without image
- Minimal design vs. branded design
- Full-screen overlay vs. corner popup

---

## 15. Advanced Tests

### Holdout Groups
- Flow vs. no flow (measure true incremental lift)

### Sequential Testing
- Test subject line → implement winner → test CTA → implement winner → test layout

### Multivariate Testing
- Test multiple variables simultaneously (requires very large audience)
