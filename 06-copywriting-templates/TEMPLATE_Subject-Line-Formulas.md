> This document provides a collection of proven subject line formulas that can be adapted to fit various email campaigns and brand voices. The goal of a subject line is to stand out in a crowded inbox and get the email opened.

# Subject Line Formulas & Best Practices

---

## Core Principles

*   **Keep it Short & Punchy:** Aim for 50 characters or less to avoid getting cut off on mobile devices.
*   **Create Curiosity:** Pique the reader's interest without giving everything away.
*   **State the Benefit:** Clearly communicate the value inside the email.
*   **Use Personalization:** Include the customer's name or reference their past behavior.
*   **Use Emojis (Sparingly):** Emojis can help your subject line stand out, but use them in a way that is on-brand.

---

## The Formulas

### 1. The Question

*   **Formula:** Ask a question that your target avatar wants to know the answer to.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Tired of [Pain Point]?"
    *   "Ready for a change?"
    *   "Did you see what's new?"

### 2. The "How-To"

*   **Formula:** Promise a solution to a problem.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "How to [Achieve Desired Outcome]"
    *   "How to get the most out of your [Product]"

### 3. The Benefit

*   **Formula:** Clearly state the primary benefit of opening the email.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Your ticket to [Desired Outcome]"
    *   "The secret to [Achieving Goal]"
    *   "Finally, a [Product Category] that works"

### 4. The Urgency/Scarcity

*   **Formula:** Create a fear of missing out (FOMO).
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Last chance: 20% off ends tonight"
    *   "Limited stock alert!"
    *   "Your cart expires soon"

### 5. The Announcement

*   **Formula:** Announce something new and exciting.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "It's here! Our new [Product] has arrived."
    *   "Big news from [Brand Name]"
    *   "You're invited: Exclusive VIP event"

### 6. The Social Proof

*   **Formula:** Leverage the power of the crowd.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "See why everyone loves our [Product]"
    *   "Don't just take our word for it..."
    *   "Join [Number] happy customers"

### 7. The Personal

*   **Formula:** Make it feel like a one-to-one message.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "A special gift for you, [Customer Name]"
    *   "I thought you might like this..."
    *   "Your recent order"

*   **Statement vs. Question:** "Your weekend outfit is here" vs. "Found your weekend outfit?"
*   **Short vs. Long:** "Big news" vs. "Big news: Our new collection just dropped"
