> This document provides best practices for creating compelling Call to Action (CTA) buttons in emails. A strong CTA is critical for driving clicks and achieving the goal of the email.

# CTA Best Practices

---

## Core Principles

*   **Be Clear & Concise:** The CTA text should be short, direct, and unambiguous.
*   **Use Action-Oriented Language:** Start with a verb that encourages action.
*   **Create a Sense of Urgency:** Use words that imply immediacy.
*   **Make it Visible:** The CTA button should be visually distinct from the rest of the email.

---

## CTA Copy Formulas

### 1. The Command

*   **Formula:** [Verb] [Noun]
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Shop Now"
    *   "Get Yours"
    *   "Learn More"

### 2. The Benefit

*   **Formula:** [Verb] [Benefit]
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Start Saving"
    *   "Get Glowing Skin"
    *   "Claim Your Discount"

### 3. The "I Want To..."

*   **Formula:** Write the CTA from the customer's perspective.
*   **Examples:**
    *   "Yes, I Want 10% Off!"
    *   "Take Me to the Sale"
    *   "I Want to Learn More"

---

## Design Best Practices

*   **Contrasting Color:** The button color should stand out from the background.
*   **Large & Legible Text:** The text on the button should be easy to read.
*   **Ample White Space:** Give the button room to breathe.
*   **Mobile-Friendly:** The button should be large enough to be easily tapped on a mobile device.

---

## A/B Testing Ideas

*   **Text:** "Shop Now" vs. "Shop the Collection"
*   **Color:** Red vs. Green
*   **Placement:** Top of the email vs. bottom of the email
*   **Shape:** Rounded corners vs. square corners
